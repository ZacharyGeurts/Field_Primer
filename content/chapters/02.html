Introduction — three dimensions of state

Chapter 2 maps three dimensions of state — our operational reading of “Reality is 3D.” This is not cosmology. It is the minimum honest coordinate system for texels, die bytes, and packet sentences that occupy addressable, readable, writable space.

Most rendering textbooks stop at pixels. Most security textbooks stop at packets. Field Technology refuses the split: offense and defense are the same literacy applied to different writable surfaces. If you cannot name the address, you cannot defend it. If you cannot predict neighbor response, you cannot dispatch responsibly.

Chapter 1 named three field families. This chapter names three mathematical postures toward those families — scalar, vector, telemetry — and three physical scales — GPU analog fabric, Field Die address space, packet field topology.
